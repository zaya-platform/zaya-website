# 10 · Hero & Imagery Brief (photo direction)

**Why this exists.** Free-licensed (Wikimedia Commons) photography does **not** contain a
modern-Ethiopian-shop/supermarket-owner image that is both on-persona and
representation-neutral. Two searches confirmed it. The build tool used here **cannot
generate photorealistic AI images**. So the authentic hero must come from **founder-supplied
imagery** — commissioned or AI-generated — and this brief specs it so it drops in with zero rework.

## Founder concern that governs all imagery (2026-07-08)
The prior hero (a street-café scene) read as **one group** rather than an inclusive picture
of Ethiopian retail, and it was **street trade**, not the shop/supermarket **owner** persona.
Every hero image must be: **(1)** a modern shop / mini-market / supermarket **owner** context,
**(2)** representation-neutral or intentionally balanced (no single religious/ethnic read),
**(3)** authentically Ethiopian, **(4)** rights-clean.

## Exact image slots (so any image drops straight in)
| Slot | Aspect / size | Subject |
|---|---|---|
| **Hero** | landscape, ≥ 2000×1200, focal subject right-of-centre (text sits left) | A confident modern **shopkeeper/owner** in their shop or mini-market, or a clean supermarket interior. Warm daylight. |
| **"Daily grind" pain** | landscape ~1600×1000 | Owner at the counter juggling stock + a paper credit notebook / phone — the recording struggle. |
| **"Who ZAYA is for"** | landscape ~1600×1000 | A supermarket / well-stocked mini-market interior, owner present, aspirational-but-real. |

## AI-generation prompts (ready to paste into any image tool)
> Style for all: photorealistic, natural warm daylight, documentary-editorial, shallow depth
> of field, authentic **Ethiopian** setting (Addis Ababa), no text/logos/watermarks, no
> Western storefront cues. Balanced, respectful, non-stereotyped.

1. **Hero —** "Photorealistic editorial photo of a confident Ethiopian shop owner (30s–40s)
   standing in the doorway of their well-stocked neighbourhood mini-market in Addis Ababa,
   holding a smartphone, warm morning light, shelves of everyday goods softly blurred behind,
   welcoming expression, space on the left of the frame for text, no visible brand logos."
2. **Daily grind —** "Photorealistic photo of an Ethiopian shopkeeper at the counter of a busy
   small shop, one hand on a paper credit notebook and a smartphone in the other, mild stress,
   stacked stock around, natural indoor light — the struggle of tracking credit and stock by hand."
3. **Who ZAYA is for —** "Photorealistic interior of a clean modern Ethiopian supermarket /
   mini-market, well-organised shelves, the owner checking stock with a phone, bright and
   aspirational yet clearly local to Addis Ababa."

> Generate 3–4 variations each; pick the most natural, least 'AI-looking'. Faces should read
> as real Ethiopians of no single group. Avoid over-saturation and plastic skin.

## Best option of all: commissioned pilot photos
Original photos of the **real Haile Garment / Jemo pilot shops and owners** beat both stock
and AI — genuine, rights-clean, exactly on persona, and they double as proof. A half-day
phone/DSLR shoot of 2–3 pilot shops (owner at counter, shelves, a sale on the ZAYA app)
covers every slot on this page and future pages.

## Interim (until real/AI images arrive)
The hero shows a **neutral, representation-safe** treatment — brand radar over the ZAYA
gradient, or a products/shelves-only supermarket interior — never the group-skewed café scene.
No rights-pending or off-persona photo ships in a publish build (`check-publish.mjs` enforces).
